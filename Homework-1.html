Question 1:

Supervised learning is defined as For each observation of the predictor measurement(s) xi, i = 1, . . . ,n there is an associated response measurement yi (An introduction to statistical learning 37).

By contrast, unsupervised learning describes the somewhat more challenging situation in which for every observation i = 1, . . . ,n, we observe a vector of measurements xi but no associated response yi (37).

The difference is that the former has observable response variables while the latter does not.
